02

Core Workouts With Your Dumbbells

🔴 Grip & Wrist Power

Dumbbell Wrist Curl

⭐ Flexor Carpi · Finger Flexors

Sit on a chair, rest forearm on thigh with palm up. Let the 20 lb dumbbell roll to fingertips, then curl the wrist up fully. Full ROM — don't cheat the bottom. This is the foundational arm wrestling move.

5×15 REPS

Reverse Wrist Curl

⭐ Extensor Group · Brachioradialis

Same position but palm facing DOWN. Curl the wrist upward. Crucial for wrist stability and preventing injury under pressure. Use 20 lb.

4×15 REPS

Dumbbell Hold Static Grip

Crushing Grip · All Finger Flexors

Hold the 30 lb dumbbell at the end of the handle (not the center) for maximum leverage challenge. Hold for 30–60 seconds. Switch hands. Do this over a bucket in case you drop it.

5×45s EACH

Two-Finger Dumbbell Holds

Finger Flexors · Pinch Grip

Hold 20 lb dumbbell using only your index + middle finger. Builds crushing finger strength elite arm wrestlers rely on. Start with 10-second holds.

4×10s EACH

🟡 Forearm Mass Builders

Hammer Curl

⭐ Brachioradialis · Brachialis · Biceps

Neutral grip (thumbs up). Curl the 30 lb dumbbell slow on the way down (3 seconds). This is the single best mass builder for the outer forearm muscle that dominates arm wrestling. The brachioradialis will visually pop.

5×10 REPS

Reverse Curl

⭐ Brachioradialis · Forearm Extensors

Overhand grip curl with 20 lb. Go slow. This DESTROYS the forearm in the best way. Combined with hammer curls = complete forearm development and injury prevention.

4×12 REPS

Zottman Curl

Biceps + Brachioradialis — Both Heads

Curl up with supinated grip (underhand), rotate to overhand at top, lower slowly with overhand. Hits EVERY angle of the arm in one movement. 20 lb, strict form only.

4×10 REPS

🔵 Pronation / Supination Power

Dumbbell Pronation Curl

⭐ Pronator Teres — #1 Arm Wrestling Muscle

Hold 20 lb dumbbell at one end (like a frying pan). Elbow at 90°. Rotate forearm so the weighted end goes DOWN and inward. This is the exact movement of an arm wrestling attack. Train both arms.

5×15 EACH

Supination Curl

Supinator · Biceps Long Head

Same as above but rotate outward (thumb goes up and away). Builds the defense against pronation attacks and bicep peak power for hooking.

4×15 EACH

Side Lateral Wrist Deviation

Flexor Carpi Ulnaris · Radialis

Hold dumbbell with thumb pointing forward. Move wrist left and right (ulnar and radial deviation). Builds the wrist cup — elite arm wrestlers use this to lock opponents down. Use 10–15 lb or end of 20 lb.

4×12 EACH DIRECTION

⚡ Explosive Pulling Power

Dumbbell Rows (Wide Elbow)

⭐ Back · Biceps · Shoulder — Full Pulling Chain

One arm row with 30 lb, but keep elbow flared wide (not tucked). This mimics the arm wrestling pull angle. Drive elbow back and up. The lat width you build transfers directly to lateral posting strength.

5×10 EACH

Curl to Wrist Curl Combo

Full Chain: Shoulder → Biceps → Forearm → Wrist

Bicep curl with 20 lb, and at the top of the curl, add a full wrist curl up. One fluid motion. This trains the entire pulling chain in sequence — exactly how arm wrestling force transfers.

4×12 REPS

Isometric Curl Hold

Biceps · Brachioradialis · Tendons

Hold the 30 lb dumbbell at 90° (elbow bent at right angle). Hold for 30 seconds. This BUILDS the strength-endurance needed to survive long matches. Tendons and connective tissue get bulletproofed.

5×30s EACH ARM

The Elite Secrets

WHAT WORLD CHAMPIONS KNOW THAT MOST PEOPLE NEVER FIND OUT

SECRET 01

Train The Wrist Cup — Not Just Grip

Most people train crushing grip. Elite arm wrestlers train the wrist cup — keeping the wrist bent toward your body in a flexed, pronated position under load. This position nullifies your opponent's attack before it starts. Train it with wrist curls done at the end of your range with a 3-second squeeze hold at the top. Do 10 reps of these every single day.

SECRET 02

Slow Negatives Build More Strength

Tendons and connective tissue in the forearm respond to eccentric (lowering) stress more than concentric. Lower every rep in 4–5 seconds. This is called eccentric overload. The brachioradialis and pronator teres will grow 30–40% faster when you add slow negatives. It feels boring. Champions don't care how it feels.

SECRET 03

The Edge of Plate Hold

Top arm wrestlers have freakish thumb and pinch grip. You can replicate this at home: hold the 20 lb dumbbell by pinching the flat weight plate area with your fingers and thumb — not the handle. Hold 20–30 seconds. This strengthens the thenar muscles (thumb base) and deep finger tendons that standard grip training ignores completely.

SECRET 04

Blood Flow Restriction (BFR)

Wrap a band or knee wrap just below your bicep, snug but not tight enough to stop pulse. Then do wrist curls and hammer curls with the 20 lb. The blood pooling causes massive metabolic stress and cell swelling that triggers muscle growth at 40% of normal load. Used in Japanese powerlifting labs. 4 sets × 30 reps with 30s rests. Your forearms will feel on fire. That's correct.

SECRET 05

Tension Training

On wrist curls and hammer curls, never let the weight fully decompress at the bottom. Stop just before your wrist fully opens. Keep the muscle under tension 100% of the set. This technique increases time under tension by 40% and forces the muscle to adapt without any "free rest" during the set. It's brutal and it works.

SECRET 06

The Newspaper Crumple

Take a single sheet of newspaper or paper. With one hand, crumple the entire sheet into a ball using only your fingers, no thumb allowed, as fast as possible. Then flatten and repeat 5 times per hand. This isolates the deep finger flexors and intrinsic hand muscles that no dumbbell exercise reaches. World class grapplers and arm wrestlers use this before bed every night.

SECRET 07

Sleep Is When Your Forearms Grow

Forearm tendons have very low blood supply compared to muscles — they heal and grow mostly during deep sleep when growth hormone peaks. If you sleep under 7 hours, tendon adaptation drops by 50%. Eat protein before bed (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, eggs). Supplement with Vitamin C (1000mg/day) — it is the precursor to collagen. No secret program beats this.

SECRET 08

The Arm Wrestling Isometric Wall Drill

Stand sideways next to a door frame. Grip the frame at elbow height in an arm wrestling start position. Pull with 100% maximum force for 6–8 seconds. Rest 10 seconds. Repeat 5–6 times per arm. This is pure neurological strength training — it teaches your CNS to fire MORE motor units at once. Strength gains happen within 2 weeks. World champions do this 3× per week.

SECRET 09

Train Both Arms Equally

Training your non-dominant arm increases neural drive and strength in your dominant arm by 7–12% through a phenomenon called cross-education. This is documented in sports science. Arm wrestlers who train both arms have stronger dominant arms than those who only train one. Every exercise, both arms, equal reps.

SECRET 10

Progressive Overload

None of these exercises matter if you don't add reps or resistance every 1–2 weeks. Track every workout. Add 1 rep to each set before adding weight. When you can do 20 lb wrist curls for 5×15 perfectly, do 5×20. Then switch to 30 lb for 5×10. Your body only adapts when it is forced to. The journal is the most important piece of equipment you own.
